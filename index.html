Biography

Foo Fighters is an American rock band, formed in Seattle, Washington, in 1994. Dave Grohl whom was once part of the band Nirvana started a one-man project after the dissolution of Nirvana, which is now known as the Foo Fighters. The group got its name from the UFOs and different aerial phenomena that were reported by Allied aircraft pilots in WW2.

Foo Fighters have been described as grunge, alternative rock, post-grunge and hard rock. When Grohl first began the band, it was being compared to his previous group "Nirvana". Grohl confirmed that Kurt Cobain was a major influence on his songwriting. They are also known for their technique of shifting between quiet verses and loud choruses, as well as rythmitic guitar riffs and drums.

They have recorded multiple albums; The Colour and the Shape, There is Nothing Left to Lose, One by One, In Your Honor, Echoes, Silence, Patience & Grace, Greatest Hits, Wasting Light, Sonic Highways, Concrete and Gold and are currently working on their tenth studio album, which will be out for the band's 25th anniversary.
